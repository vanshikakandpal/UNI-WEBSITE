About Princeton University

Founded in 1746, Princeton is the fourth-oldest college in the United States and a world leader in education, research, and innovation.

Our Legacy

Princeton has produced U.S. presidents, Nobel laureates, Fields Medalists, and leaders across academia, business, and public service. With a strong liberal arts foundation and a commitment to groundbreaking research, Princeton stands as a beacon of excellence worldwide.

Quick Facts

Campus

600 acres of stunning Gothic architecture and state-of-the-art research facilities.

Ranked #1

Consistently ranked #1 National University by U.S. News & World Report.

Student Life

A vibrant community with 300+ clubs, athletics, arts, and cultural events.

Our Mission

Princeton is dedicated to advancing learning through scholarship, research, and teaching of unsurpassed quality, with a focus on preparing students for leadership and service to humanity.
