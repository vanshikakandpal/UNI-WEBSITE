Computer Science Department

Advancing innovation, research, and teaching in computing, AI, data science, and systems to transform technology and society.

About the Department

The Department of Computer Science at Princeton is a leader in advancing computational thinking, programming, systems, and AI. It offers undergraduate, graduate, and research opportunities that focus on both the theoretical foundations and practical applications of computing. Students gain strong skills in algorithms, machine learning, programming, and interdisciplinary problem-solving.

Courses Offered

  • COS 126 – Computer Science: An Interdisciplinary Approach
  • COS 217 – Introduction to Programming Systems
  • COS 226 – Algorithms and Data Structures
  • COS 324 – Introduction to Machine Learning
  • COS 333 – Advanced Programming Techniques
  • COS 418 – Distributed Systems
  • COS 426 – Computer Graphics
  • COS 461 – Computer Networks
  • COS 495 – Special Topics in Artificial Intelligence

Degree & Requirements

Undergraduate students explore a mix of theory and practice, from algorithms to systems programming. Graduate students pursue advanced research in artificial intelligence, cryptography, machine learning, robotics, and computational biology. Independent work and senior theses are core components of the Princeton computer science curriculum.
