English Department

Exploring literature, language, and critical thought to understand cultural traditions and contemporary issues.

About the Department

The English Department at Princeton is dedicated to the study of literature across periods, genres, and traditions. Students develop analytical, critical, and creative skills through the study of poetry, prose, drama, and theory. The program emphasizes writing, critical thinking, and engagement with diverse literary traditions.

Courses Offered

  • ENG 200 – Introduction to Literary Theory
  • ENG 215 – Shakespeare: Comedies and Histories
  • ENG 220 – American Literature: Origins to 1865
  • ENG 245 – Modern Poetry
  • ENG 310 – Contemporary Fiction
  • ENG 325 – African American Literature
  • ENG 360 – Postcolonial Literature
  • ENG 420 – Literary Theory and Criticism
  • ENG 480 – Senior Seminar in English Studies

Degree & Requirements

English majors complete foundational courses in literary traditions, theory, and writing, along with electives across diverse fields. Independent work, junior papers, and the senior thesis allow students to pursue research in areas like modernism, cultural studies, or medieval literature. Graduate students engage in advanced literary scholarship and teaching.
