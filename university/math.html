Mathematics Department

Fostering discovery, rigor, and creativity in pure and applied mathematics to shape the foundations of science and society.

About the Department

Princeton’s Mathematics Department is internationally renowned for its contributions to both research and teaching. It offers an AB undergraduate program and advanced graduate studies, emphasizing deep theoretical understanding, creative problem solving, and interdisciplinary collaboration. From number theory and geometry to applied mathematics and mathematical physics, the department cultivates excellence at all levels.

  • MAT 216 – Linear Algebra with Applications
  • MAT 305 – Mathematical Logic
  • MAT 320 – Real Analysis
  • MAT 322 – Complex Analysis
  • MAT 330 – Abstract Algebra
  • MAT 335 – Geometry
  • MAT 425 – Partial Differential Equations
  • MAT 451 – Topology

Degree & Requirements

The undergraduate program emphasizes proof-based courses, independent research, and broad exposure to different fields of mathematics. Students complete core courses in analysis, algebra, and geometry/topology, alongside electives spanning applied mathematics, number theory, or mathematical physics. Graduate students pursue original research, contributing to Princeton’s longstanding tradition of mathematical excellence.
